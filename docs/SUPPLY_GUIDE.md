# SUPPLY GUIDE — BOM Ingest, Box Assignment, and Individual Items

Audience: supply sergeant or armorer responsible for uploading BOM PDFs, assigning equipment to boxes, and handling edge cases (zero-on-hand, individual items, exclusions).

---

## What is a BOM?

A BOM (Bill of Material) represents one end item — for example, one M4A1 Carbine with its serial number — plus all of its component line items. The tool reads BOM PDFs and creates one BOM card per end item.

BOM PDFs are generated by the property book system or unit supply. Each filename encodes the item: model, LIN, serial number, and (optionally) bumper number.

---

## Step 1 — Uploading BOM PDFs

During Step 3 (PACKING) of the operator workflow:

1. Click **Upload BOM PDFs** in the right panel, or drag and drop files onto the upload zone.
2. Drop all BOM PDFs for this connex at once — the tool parses them in bulk.
3. Each BOM becomes a card in the right tray, showing:
   - **Nomenclature** — the item name (e.g. RIFLE M4A1)
   - **LIN** — Line Item Number
   - **NSN** — National Stock Number (sniffed from the PDF if present)
   - **Serial** — serial number from the filename

The parser is shape-based and order-independent — it classifies each filename token by its format (LIN = 6-char alpha-numeric, serial = long alphanumeric string, etc.) rather than by position. Cards with missing LIN or Model are flagged for review.

**What if a BOM card shows missing data?** The BOM tray highlights cards with a warning state. Click the card to edit the LIN or nomenclature before assigning.

---

## Step 2 — Assigning BOMs to boxes

Each BOM card must be assigned to exactly one box in the connex.

**3D view (drag and drop):**
1. Drag a BOM card from the right tray.
2. Hover over a box in the 3D scene — it highlights gold.
3. Drop to assign.

**List/table view (click to assign):**
1. Click a BOM card to select it.
2. Click the target box row in the assignment table.

**Assignment moves (via the API, also accessible in the UI):**

| Move type | What it does |
|-----------|-------------|
| `{bom_id, box_num}` | Assign BOM to a specific box number |
| `{bom_id, separate: true}` | Pull this BOM into its own dedicated box (next free box number) |
| `{bom_id, exclude: true}` | Remove this BOM from the packing list entirely |

Use **separate** for sensitive items or items that need their own DD1750. Use **exclude** for items that are not shipping in this connex (deadlined, retained, etc.).

---

## Step 3 — Filling SLOC and SHRH POC

After assigning BOMs, click each box to open its detail panel and fill:

- **SLOC** — Storage Location Code. Where is this box physically located? (e.g. `BLDG-100`, `YARD-A3`, `ISO PAD 2`). This is required before the connex can be sealed.
- **SHRH POC** — Sub-Hand Receipt Holder, Point of Contact. Who is accountable for the items in this box? Enter the name in RANK LAST, FIRST format (e.g. `CPT JONES`). Required before sealing.

A box turns **green** when it has at least one BOM and both SLOC and SHRH POC are filled. All boxes must be green to advance to the Seal step.

## Zero-on-hand handling

A zero-on-hand condition occurs when a box's BOM items all have a quantity of zero — equipment that appears on the property book but is not physically present.

The tool handles zero-on-hand as follows:

- Zero-quantity items are tracked but rendered on the DD1750 with a `0` quantity.
- The SITREP flags any box with all-zero quantities: `"Box N has zero-on-hand items."` (or similar).
- Zero-on-hand is **not** a seal blocker. The connex can be sealed with zero-on-hand boxes as long as SLOC and SHRH POC are filled.
- The S4 must reconcile zero-on-hand items against the property book before certifying the packing list.

**Why does the tool not block sealing on zero-on-hand?** Because zero-on-hand is a property book discrepancy to be resolved, not a packing error. The PDF documents the condition; the SITREP flags it for the S4.

## Individual items

Individual items are loose items not covered by BOM PDFs — tools, accessories, non-standard components, or items too few to have their own BOM.

Add them at Step 5 (INDIVIDUAL ITEMS) after sealing:

1. Click a box in the panel.
2. Click **Add Item**.
3. Fill any combination of:
   - **Description** — free text (e.g. "SLING ASSY, SINGLE POINT")
   - **SN** — serial number
   - **NSN** — 13-digit National Stock Number
   - **LIN** — 6-character Line Item Number
4. All fields are optional. An item with only a description is valid.

Individual items appear on the DD1750 for that box and in the SITREP individual item count.

> **Fast-follow note:** A planned AI helper (owl-alpha) will suggest NSN and LIN values from a description. The hook is present in the form but is not connected in the current release.

## Box state color reference

| Color | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| Gray (empty) | No BOMs or individual items assigned |
| Amber (warning) | Has content but SLOC or SHRH POC is missing |
| Green (complete) | Has content, SLOC filled, SHRH POC filled — ready to seal |

---

## Generating DD1750s

At Step 6 (CLOSE & STAMP), clicking **Generate DD1750s** produces a ZIP file containing one PDF per occupied box.

Each PDF includes:
- Full header: SLOC, SHRH POC, SUN, CONNEX #, SEAL #, Packed By, Signed By, Date
- Battalion stamp from the profile's Stamp Text
- All BOM rows with nomenclature, LIN, NSN, serial, and quantity
- Individual items appended after BOM rows
- Page pagination with `NOTHING FOLLOWS` marker on the last page

Boxes with zero items are not included in the ZIP.

---

## Re-generating after changes

If you need to update an individual item or add a late BOM after sealing:

1. Use `PUT /api/connex/<connex_id>` to update the box's individual_items.
2. Re-run `POST /api/connex/<connex_id>/generate` to download a new ZIP.

The connex status remains `sealed`. Re-generating does not change the seal timestamp or invalidate the seal validation.

## Troubleshooting ingest

**BOM card shows no LIN or model:** The filename could not be parsed. Edit the card manually in the tray or rename the file to include the LIN (format: `A12345`) and re-upload.

**Ingest returns no cards:** The PDF may be image-only (scanned). The tool attempts OCR if tesseract is installed; otherwise, the PDF is skipped. Check the server logs for `OCR_AVAILABLE` status.

**Cards disappear after server restart:** BOM ingest state is in-memory. Re-upload BOM PDFs after any restart. Connex and profile JSON files are persisted to disk and will be there when the server comes back up.

**Too many boxes / want to reduce box count:** You cannot reduce the box count after creation without starting a new connex. Exclude BOMs from extra boxes (`{bom_id, exclude: true}`) or use **separate** to reassign — the PDF generator skips empty boxes.
